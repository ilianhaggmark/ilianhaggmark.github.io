The aim of this simple homepage is to briefly present me and my research, but also to serve as a sandbox for HTML/CSS/JS/SVG.

Research

I'm a researcher at The University of Tokyo working with X-ray imaging. My work focuses on phase-contrast methods for improved image quality of biological samples. This is done both through experimental work on lab sources and synchrotron sources and with simulations. The research is carried out under a JSPS Fellowship in the research group of Prof. Tekenori Sasaki at the University Museum (東京大学総合研究博物館).

Soft skills
Although there is a stereotype of research as a solitary profession, that is not the case. Research have taught me about project management, leadership, supervision, collaboration (with your office neighbor and people around the world). The product is also all about communicating ideas, whether it is through a public presentation, images or text.
Teaching
I started teaching as an undergraduate and have done it ever since. I have taught mainly optics and classical electro-magnetism, but also calculus. As a doctoral student, I have supervised students for BSc and MSc thesis projects. I think teaching is very rewarding as a way to “give back”, but also great to gain new understanding of a subject. My interest in teaching also connects to a broader interest in technical and scientific communication. This stretches from the quite formal like terminology to how to tell a compelling story for a general audience.
